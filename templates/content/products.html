Holdings Verification

Access Checking & Link Fault Detection

Holdings verification usually involves the systematic crawling of an entire catalog. This process artificially inflates usage, which weakens the ability to negotiate by decreasing cost per download.

Passive, Local Observation

The AUDITOR observes genuine activity as it occurs yielding no unnatural usage inflation. Inaccessibility that is isolated to sub-networks is quickly identified.

Comprehensive, Perpetual Inspection

The AUDITOR is truly comprehensive. There is no statistical sampling and the verification is consistent and continual.

Real-time Holdings Inference

Sophisticated click-stream analysis infers library holdings in real-time. Unexpected access denials are detected immediately.

Usage Logging

Comprehensive, Granular, and Local

Currently, less than half of all e-resources offer standardized reports and a third offer no reports at all. The reporting that is available consumes a great deal of time delivering only general statistics when more granular reporting is needed.

Exhaustive, Detailed Data

The AUDITOR does not require usage data from publishers. It logs usage at the article level and in much more detail.

Real-time Consolidation

Rather than waiting weeks, usage can be reviewed within minutes.

Internal Distribution & Local Archival

Internal IP addresses allow usage to be attributed departmentally. The AUDITOR is its own local archive. All usage in full detail is always available.

Availability Monitoring

Passive, Comparative Analysis

E-resources are not always available and their performance can vary dramatically. As with holdings verification, monitoring e-resources by systematically requesting content weakens the ability to negotiate by artificially inflating usage. Outages are difficult to diagnose, consuming valuable resources when they occur.

Passive, Continuous Observation

The AUDITOR observes genuine activity as it occurs yielding no unnatural usage inflation. Every request is analyzed.

Comparative Troubleshooting

Error reports from an affected institution are anonymously combined with those from others to immediately determine if the issue is localized or widespread. All affected institutions will be promptly notified of the nature of the outage.
